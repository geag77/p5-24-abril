Where Do People Drink The Most Beer in America?

On Friday, I responded to a question from a reader who wanted to know how many people had, like him, never had a drink of alcohol. After looking at trends within the U.S., I compared the percentage of lifetime abstainers in countries across the world using data from the World Health Organization (WHO). Luckily, being a health organization, WHO isn’t just interested in who is and isn’t drinking — it wants to know what’s in their glasses and how much they’re knocking back. It has national data on average alcohol consumption per capita for those age 15 and over in each country. The data also describes consumption by beverage type: wine, beer, spirits and “other.” To break consumption down by beverage, WHO again used government records as well as statistics from the alcohol industry and the United Nation’s Food and Agriculture Organization database.
